Heritage Sites of Karnataka

Journey Through the Annals of a Glorious Past

A Land of Empires and Artistry

Karnataka's history is a rich tapestry woven by mighty empires like the Hoysalas, Chalukyas, and the Vijayanagara Empire. Their legacy is etched in stone across the state, from magnificent temple complexes to imposing forts and opulent palaces, making it a dream destination for history buffs and art lovers.

Iconic Heritage Destinations

Hampi

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Hampi was the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire. Its surreal landscape is dotted with hundreds of ancient temples, ruins, and monuments.

Key Attractions: Virupaksha Temple, Stone Chariot

Belur & Halebidu

These twin towns showcase the pinnacle of Hoysala architecture, with intricately carved temples that are masterpieces of stone craftsmanship.

Key Attractions: Chennakesava Temple, Hoysaleswara Temple

Mysuru Palace

The magnificent seat of the Kingdom of Mysore, this palace is a stunning example of Indo-Saracenic architecture, especially when illuminated at night.

Key Attractions: Durbar Hall, Golden Howdah

Badami, Aihole & Pattadakal

Known as the cradle of Indian temple architecture, this circuit features ancient rock-cut cave temples and structural temples from the Chalukya dynasty.

Key Attractions: Badami Caves, Durga Temple

Shravanabelagola

A prominent Jain pilgrimage center, famous for the colossal monolithic statue of Gommateshwara Bahubali, one of the largest of its kind in the world.

Key Attractions: Gommateshwara Statue

Best Time to Visit

The ideal time to explore Karnataka's heritage sites is during the cooler, drier months from **October to February**. The pleasant weather is perfect for walking around large temple complexes and archaeological sites.
