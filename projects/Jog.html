Jog Falls

The Majestic Plunge of the Sharavathi

A Spectacle of Nature

Jog Falls, created by the Sharavathi River, is India's second-highest plunge waterfall. It is a breathtaking spectacle where the river splits into four distinct cascades – Raja, Rani, Roarer, and Rocket – which thunder down a cliff from a height of 253 meters. The falls are at their most magnificent during the monsoon season, attracting tourists from all over the world.

Height

253 meters (830 ft)

River

Sharavathi River

Best Time to Visit

August to December

The Four Cascades

Raja (King)

The highest and most majestic of the four, plunging directly to the base without interruption.

Rani (Queen)

Flows gracefully and quietly over the rocks, resembling a dancing lady.

Roarer

This cascade makes a loud, powerful noise as it gushes down onto the rocks.

Rocket

Named for its appearance, as it shoots downwards in a series of jets.
