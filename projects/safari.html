Karnataka Wildlife Safaris

Explore the Wild Heart of Southern India

A Realm of Rich Biodiversity

Karnataka is a treasure trove for wildlife enthusiasts, boasting one of the highest tiger and elephant populations in India. Its diverse ecosystems, from dense forests to sprawling grasslands, are protected within numerous national parks and wildlife sanctuaries. Embark on a thrilling safari to witness majestic animals in their natural habitats.

Top Safari Destinations

Bandipur National Park

Part of the Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve, Bandipur is one of India's premier tiger reserves with a significant population of elephants, gaurs, and sloth bears.

Key Wildlife: Tigers, Elephants, Leopards, Dholes

Nagarhole National Park

Famous for the Kabini backwaters, Nagarhole offers incredible sightings of large mammals and is one of the best places to spot the elusive black panther.

Key Wildlife: Leopards, Tigers, Black Panther, Crocodiles

Biligiriranga Hills (BRT)

A unique ecological bridge between the Western and Eastern Ghats, offering a diverse range of flora and fauna, including the rare Malabar giant squirrel.

Key Wildlife: Elephants, Gaurs, Sambars, Giant Squirrels

Dandeli Wildlife Sanctuary

A birdwatcher's paradise with over 200 species, including the Malabar pied hornbill. Also known for its black panthers and adventure sports.

Key Wildlife: Hornbills, Black Panther, Bison

Bhadra Tiger Reserve

Located in the lush hills of Chikmagalur, Bhadra is known for its thriving tiger population, boat safaris, and picturesque landscapes.

Key Wildlife: Tigers, Leopards, Gaurs, Crocodiles

Best Time for a Safari

The ideal time to visit most wildlife sanctuaries in Karnataka is during the dry season, from **October to May**. During these months, water sources are limited, increasing the chances of spotting animals as they gather near lakes and rivers.
