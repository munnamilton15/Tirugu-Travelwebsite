Karnataka's Hill Stations

Discover the Serene Escapes of the Western Ghats

Misty Mountains and Coffee Plantations

Cradled by the Western Ghats, Karnataka is home to some of South India's most enchanting hill stations. These cool, verdant retreats offer a perfect escape from the city life, with their rolling hills, aromatic coffee and spice plantations, cascading waterfalls, and breathtaking viewpoints.

Top Hill Station Destinations

Coorg (Madikeri)

Often called the 'Scotland of India', Coorg is famous for its lush coffee estates, misty landscapes, and rich Kodava culture.

Key Attractions: Abbey Falls, Raja's Seat, Dubare Elephant Camp

Chikmagalur

Known as the 'Coffee Land of Karnataka', this serene destination is home to the state's highest peak, Mullayanagiri, offering stunning trekking trails.

Key Attractions: Mullayanagiri, Baba Budangiri, Hebbe Falls

Kemmangundi

A picturesque hill station with beautifully laid-out ornamental gardens, magnificent mountain views, and spectacular sunsets.

Key Attractions: Z Point, Hebbe Falls, Rose Garden

Sakleshpur

A trekker's paradise, Sakleshpur is known for its incredible Bisle Ghat viewpoint and the historic Manjarabad Fort.

Key Attractions: Bisle Viewpoint, Manjarabad Fort, Railway Trek

Agumbe

Famed for its glorious sunsets over the Arabian Sea and its dense rainforests, earning it the title 'Cherrapunji of the South'.

Key Attractions: Sunset View Point, Barkana Falls, Rainforest

Best Time to Visit

The best time to explore Karnataka's hill stations is from **September to May**. The post-monsoon months offer lush green landscapes, while the winter and spring provide pleasant weather for sightseeing and trekking.
