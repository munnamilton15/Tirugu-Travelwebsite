Waterfalls of Karnataka

Witness the Power and Beauty of Nature's Cascades

A Land of Thundering Cascades

Thanks to the mighty rivers of the Western Ghats, Karnataka is blessed with an abundance of stunning waterfalls. During the monsoon season, these cascades come alive, thundering down cliffs and valleys, creating breathtaking spectacles of nature's power and beauty.

Must-Visit Waterfalls

Jog Falls

India's second-highest plunge waterfall, where the Sharavathi River spectacularly drops in four distinct cascades: Raja, Rani, Roarer, and Rocket.

Location: Shivamogga

Shivanasamudra Falls

A majestic twin waterfall on the Kaveri river, splitting into the Gaganachukki and Bharachukki falls, creating a powerful and wide cascade.

Location: Mandya

Abbey Falls

Nestled amidst private coffee and spice estates near Madikeri, this waterfall offers a picturesque view from a hanging bridge.

Location: Coorg

Hebbe Falls

Located inside a coffee estate in Kemmangundi, this enchanting waterfall cascades down in two stages, Dodda Hebbe and Chikka Hebbe.

Location: Chikmagalur

Irupu Falls

A sacred waterfall in the Brahmagiri Range, believed to possess cleansing powers. It's a starting point for the trek to Brahmagiri Peak.

Location: Coorg

Best Time to Visit

The waterfalls are at their roaring best during the monsoon and post-monsoon months, from **June to November**. The increased water flow makes for a truly spectacular and powerful sight.
