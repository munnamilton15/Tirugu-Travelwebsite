Agumbe Rainforest

The Cherrapunji of the South

A Biodiversity Hotspot

Agumbe is a high-altitude village in the Western Ghats, renowned for its pristine rainforests, heavy rainfall, and incredible biodiversity. It is home to the King Cobra and forms a part of the Someshwara Wildlife Sanctuary. The region, famously the setting for the TV series 'Malgudi Days', offers a serene escape for nature lovers, researchers, and photographers.

Annual Rainfall

Over 7,000 mm

Known For

Rich Biodiversity, King Cobras

Best Time to Visit

November to February

Key Attractions

Sunset View Point

One of the highest points in the Western Ghats, offering spectacular sunset views over the Arabian Sea.

Barkana Falls

Among the ten highest waterfalls in India, this majestic waterfall is a sight to behold during the monsoon.

Rainforest Research Station

A unique facility for studying the ecology of the rainforest and its diverse species, especially the King Cobra.

Sample 2-Day Itinerary

Day 1: Arrival and Exploration

  • Morning departure from Shivamogga/Udupi.
  • Check into a traditional homestay in Agumbe.
  • Visit the Rainforest Research Station.
  • Head to the Sunset View Point for a mesmerizing evening.
  • Dinner featuring local Malnad cuisine.

Day 2: Waterfalls and Departure

  • After breakfast, trek to the stunning Barkana Falls.
  • Explore the serene surroundings and enjoy the natural beauty.
  • Return to the homestay for lunch.
  • Depart from Agumbe in the afternoon.
